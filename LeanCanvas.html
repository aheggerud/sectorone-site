Garage-first data ops wrapper for MoTeC • Cosworth • AiM • VBOX — NA & EU privateer focus

Problem

  • Fragmented tools & formats: Teams juggle MoTeC, Cosworth Pi, AiM, VBOX — proprietary, non-interoperable formats; steep learning curves and manual conversions.
  • Time-crunch post-session: 30–60 minutes of manual copying, syncing video ↔ telemetry, file renaming; slow “time-to-insight.”
  • No single timeline: Telemetry, video, GPS, and notes live in silos; manual alignment leads to missed context and slower driver coaching.
  • No versioning / season memory: Setups, workbooks, and baselines scattered across laptops; naming inconsistencies; learnings get lost through the season.
  • Sharing & access pain: Hard to share with coaches/guest drivers; lack of RBAC; everyone needs vendor software; mixed-hardware teams struggle.
  • Garage reliability issues: Flaky power/network; failed transfers/USBs; limited IT support at the track; need offline-first, “just works” workflows.
Outcome: decision latency, errors (wrong baselines, lost logs), and wasted engineer time.

Customer Segments

  • Primary early adopters GT3/GT4 privateers (SRO, IMSA GS, British GT, NLS, regionals) — 1–3 car teams, lean engineering staff.
  • Radical one-make, TCR, LMP3, grassroots endurance/rally (ChampCar, Lemons, 24H series) with similar workflow constraints.
  • Secondary OEM customer programs & multi-car teams; factory later.
  • Buyer: Team manager / performance engineer. Users: engineers, drivers, strategists, OEM support.
  • Geo: North America & Europe to start; series schedules dictate cadence and support model.

Unique Value Proposition

“All your race data — unified, fast, and in sync.” SectorOne is the garage-first data ops platform that wraps MoTeC/Cosworth/AiM/VBOX: a single timeline for telemetry, video, GPS & notes; instant debriefs, live AI insights, and season memory — with zero car-side changes.
  • One platform, any logger: Normalize channels/units; compare across cars/vendors without format pain.
  • Faster decisions: automate import, sync, overlays, and reports; engineers analyze instead of wrangling files.
  • Beyond telemetry: rich cross-media context improves coaching & setup choices.
  • Built for privateers: offline-capable, simple deployment, safe cloud sync later.

Solution

  • Universal ingest & normalization: watch-folders for i2/CSV/VBO/video; canonical event schema (session key, car, driver, compound, weather, timestamps); enforce units & clock.
  • Cross-media timeline: telemetry + onboard video + GPS + annotations in perfect sync; timestamped coach/driver notes, incident tags.
  • Artifact versioning: Git-like repo for setups/run sheets/workbooks; human-readable diffs; approvals; link snapshots of telemetry to each change.
  • Post-session automation: lap deltas, driver overlays, stint degradation, tire envelopes; auto-publish dashboards & one-page PDFs minutes after the flag.
  • RBAC & sharing: role spaces (driver/engineer/manager/OEM), secure links, audit logs, easy off-boarding; mobile summaries for managers.
  • Season memory: trend drift & condition sensitivity (track temp, wind, fuel load, compound); quick recall of prior event baselines.
  • Garage reliability: offline-first agent, local cache, conflict-free sync queues, prioritized uploads (telemetry before video).

  • OEM programs: partner with customer racing (Porsche, Audi, HPD) to recommend to privateers.

Cost Structure

  • Engineering: ingestion connectors (MoTeC/AiM/Cosworth/VBOX), time-series infra, UI, collaboration, QA.
  • Cloud: object storage (video, logs), TSDB, compute for reports/ML; bandwidth.
  • Trackside ops: pilot support, travel kits, demo rigs; documentation & training.
  • Sales/marketing: events, partners, content, ads; founder-led early to contain CAC.
  • Customer success: responsive support & on-call during race weekends.
  • G&A & legal: data rights, contracts, insurance; partnership fees if any.

Key Metrics

  • Time-to-report after checkered flag (goal < 10 min).
  • Adoption: active cars/teams, sessions uploaded per weekend.
  • Feature usage: % sessions with video sync, # cross-media queries, setup diff views.
  • Reliability: weekend reliability score; error/rollback rates on setups.
  • Retention & expansion: season renewals, cars per team, OEM program wins.
  • Onboarding friction: time to first upload; support ticket volume.

Unfair Advantage

  • Any-logger integration moat: broad converter/normalizer library across MoTeC/AiM/Cosworth/Bosch/VBOX — hard to replicate.
  • Unified repository & lock-in: telemetry + video + notes + setups with history; teams’ institutional memory lives here.
  • Offline-first architecture: garage-reliable edge agent + conflict-free sync; not trivial for cloud-only rivals.
  • Domain focus: built specifically for privateer workflows; faster iteration on niche needs.
  • Network effects (future): optional anonymized benchmarks/insights as user base grows.
  • Partnerships: OEM/customer-racing endorsements; reseller/consultant channel leverage.
